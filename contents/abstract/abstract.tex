{\itshape
Wind power plants using doubly fed induction generator (DFIG-WT) and permanent
magnet synchronous generator (PMSG-WT) alter small signal stability (SSS)
differently, depending on how each technology couples machine dynamics to the
grid. This study analyzes and compares the SSS impact of DFIG-WT and PMSG-WT
integration on the IEEE nine-bus test system through eigenvalue-based modal
analysis in DIgSILENT PowerFactory 2024, across eleven scenarios varying wind
turbine technology, integration bus location (Bus 7 and Bus 9), and loading
conditions. DFIG-WT integration reduces the dominant mode damping ratio from
7.22\% to approximately 4.04\% regardless of integration location, and narrows
the load stability margin: the instability threshold for Load A falls from 247\%
to 83\%, and for Load B from 373\% to 123\%, with both thresholds producing
aperiodic instability (damping ratio $-$100\%) rather than the oscillatory
instability seen in the conventional system. PMSG-WT integration holds or raises
the damping ratio to 7.25\%--8.73\%, with Load A reaching 165\% and Load B
reaching 284\% before instability, because the fully-rated converter decouples
machine dynamics from the network and leaves the dominant oscillation mode
governed by the remaining synchronous generators. Participation factor analysis
confirms that the DFIG-WT shaft variable ($\Delta\delta_{12}$) drives the
dominant oscillation mode, while the internal rotor angle ($\varphi_m$) drives
the unstable mode under increased load. DFIG-WT erodes damping and shrinks the
safe operating range; PMSG-WT preserves or improves both, with the margin gain
depending on which generator the wind plant replaces.
}
